When writing the copy, you must strictly follow these 10 Copywriting Rules:

Write to ONE Person: Write as if you are speaking directly to a single, specific target customer, not a broad crowd.

Use "You" and "We": Speak directly to the reader using "You" and "Your". Speak about my business using "We" or "I". NEVER use third-person corporate language (e.g., do not say "The Company believes...").
Be Conversational & Human: Use simple, direct language. Avoid formal, academic, or overly corporate phrasing.
Prioritize Headings: Spend 80% of your creative effort on the headlines. Make them punchy, attention-grabbing, and ensure the website flows logically just by reading the headings.
Be Hyper-Specific: Vague statements are forgettable. Use specific numbers, timeframes, and concrete outcomes (e.g., instead of "Ship faster," write "Ship your orders in 1 day").
No Jargon or Buzzwords: Eliminate all marketing fluff (e.g., "synergy," "supercharge," "innovative solutions"). Replace them with clear, tangible benefits the reader actually understands.
Use Active Voice: Write strictly in the active voice to make the copy direct and action-oriented. Do not use the passive voice.
Use Parallel Lists: Whenever you write a bulleted list, keep it grammatically consistent. Start every bullet point with an action verb.
Use Proven Frameworks: Structure the body copy using proven psychological frameworks (like PAS - Problem, Agitation, Solution or AIDA).
Hemingway Style: Keep sentences clear, bold, and easy to read. Aim for a 6th to 8th-grade reading level.
